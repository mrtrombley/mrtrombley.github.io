About Me


About Me

     Hello! My name is Matt Trombley. Thanks for stopping by my website. I am currently a second-semester sophomore at Clemson University in Clemson, South Carolina. My major is a Bachelor of Science in Mathematical Sciences with a concentration in statistics. I originally started out majoring in mechanical engineering before switching over in the Spring of 2016, so I have some experience in engineering fields (ex. statics, dynamics, mechanics of materials, engineering graphics, and electrical engineering). I hope to get into analytics or data science as my career so I'm working on my degree in statistics while self-teaching coding skills on the side. In the next few years I am planning on teaching myself SQL, Python, Hadoop, as well as improving my R and SAS skills. Some of the classes I'll be taking in the coming years are:


  • Introduction to Statistical Theory
  • Sampling Theory and Methods
  • Regression and Time-Series Analysis
  • Linear Programming
  • Introduction to Stochastic Models

     I have already taken classes on statistical programming and theory of probability. I'm planning on doing some analytics projects which will be posted on my site in the coming months. If you're interested in those check out the "Posts" page in the navigation bar above.

     Here at Clemson, I'm involved with the IPTAY Student Advisory Board. This board serves as the liaison between the Clemson student body and IPTAY, Clemson's athletic booster organization. This organization is one of the largest in the nation. I am on the Vendor Benefits Committee, working to increase relations between local businesses and IPTAY's Collegiate Club. The organization promotes many sports and student-athletes on campus, as well as runs events to benefit student IPTAY members.
